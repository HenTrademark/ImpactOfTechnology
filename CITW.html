Computers in the workforce

Throughout the past few years, we have seen a growth in the use of computers in industry. Nearly every company uses one in someway, whether that be for scheduling or storage. Taking this all in mind, this begs the question: How will computers impact our jobs?

Examples of use

The amount of jobs that are being done by computers is ever-increasing. A few are listed below:

  • Cashiers. Computers are manning self-service machines, which took over cashiers
  • Bank Tellers. Automated Teller Machines (ATMs) took over bank tellers
  • Phone Operators. Automated phone calls took over phone operators
  • Warehouse movers. Robots have been able to move items around warehouses with ease
  • Bowling ball pinsetters Some time ago robots took over this job from people who'd sit next to the pins and pick them up

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Improve efficiency. Companies like Amazon use these machines to reduce delivery times and speed up the process of manufacturing for the company
  • Reduces the strain on workers. Computers can do a task for a long time without dropping its performance, so replace humans with them

Cons

  • Loss of employment. Computers taking over jobs which were done by humans before means that the people in those jobs don't have jobs anymore
  • Very high dependence. If anything happens to any of the computers then bad things could happen (e.g. doxxing, loss in output, loss in revenue, etc.)
